Portfolio-level judgment: "invest when a valuable decision can improve, evidence and ownership exist, and the organization can operate the system. Pause when the use case lacks a measurable outcome or accountable owner." The `opportunity_score()` code (weighted value/feasibility/readiness/risk, explicitly *not* a replacement for accountable judgment) is a simplified teaching version of the same instinct behind this session's Opportunity Radar Decision Card — score, but don't let the score make the call alone (see the Decision Card's own "why now" prose reasoning, not just its 1-5 scores).

The failure list — "strategy by demo count," "governance detached from delivery," "ROI without baselines" — reads like a direct rebuke of treating a Radar report's ranking table as a decision by itself, rather than a starting point for the kind of reasoning this session did before picking one opportunity to pursue.
